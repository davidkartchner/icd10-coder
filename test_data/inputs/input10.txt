A 52-year-old male presents to the ED with severe epigastric pain radiating to the back, nausea, and vomiting for the past 12 hours. History significant for heavy alcohol use and fatty food consumption the night before. Vital signs: T 38.2°C, BP 135/85 mmHg, HR 102 bpm. Physical exam reveals epigastric tenderness with guarding. Labs show lipase 1200 U/L, amylase 450 U/L, WBC 16,500/μL, Ca 7.8 mg/dL, BUN 45 mg/dL. CT abdomen shows pancreatic inflammation with peripancreatic fluid and no evidence of gallstones. Patient admitted for acute pancreatitis management.
