A 42-year-old female presents with progressive joint pain, morning stiffness lasting >2 hours, and fatigue for the past 6 months. Physical exam reveals bilateral synovitis of MCPs, PIPs, and wrists with ulnar deviation. Both knees are warm and swollen. Labs show RF positive (75 IU/mL), anti-CCP positive (>200 U/mL), ESR 55 mm/hr, CRP 3.8 mg/dL. X-rays of hands show periarticular osteopenia and early erosive changes in several MCP joints. ANA negative. Patient also reports occasional dry eyes and mouth.
