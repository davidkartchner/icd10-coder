A 45-year-old female presents to the emergency department with acute onset of severe chest pain radiating to the left arm, which started 2 hours ago while at rest. The pain is described as crushing and is associated with shortness of breath, nausea, and profuse sweating. Patient has a history of hypertension and type 2 diabetes mellitus. Vital signs show BP 160/95 mmHg, HR 102 bpm, RR 24/min, O2 sat 94% on room air. Physical examination reveals an anxious patient in acute distress. Cardiac examination shows regular rhythm with S4 gallop. ECG shows ST-segment elevation in leads V1-V4. Initial troponin I is elevated at 2.3 ng/mL. Patient was immediately taken to the cardiac catheterization lab where 100% occlusion of the left anterior descending artery was found and successfully treated with PCI and stent placement.
