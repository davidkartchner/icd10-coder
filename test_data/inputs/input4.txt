A 34-year-old female presents with sudden onset severe abdominal pain in the right lower quadrant that started 12 hours ago. The pain started periumbilically and migrated to the RLQ. She reports nausea, vomiting, and loss of appetite. No diarrhea or urinary symptoms. Last menstrual period was 2 weeks ago. Vital signs: T 38.1°C, BP 122/76 mmHg, HR 88 bpm, RR 18/min. Physical exam reveals rebound tenderness and guarding in the RLQ, positive McBurney's point tenderness. WBC count is 14,500/μL with neutrophilia. CT abdomen shows enlarged appendix with surrounding inflammation. Patient was taken to surgery for laparoscopic appendectomy.
