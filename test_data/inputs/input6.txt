A 68-year-old female presents with acute onset left-sided weakness and difficulty speaking that started 45 minutes ago while watching TV. Past medical history includes hypertension, atrial fibrillation (not on anticoagulation), and hyperlipidemia. Vital signs: BP 178/95 mmHg, HR 88 irregular, RR 16/min, O2 sat 98%. NIHSS score is 14. Physical exam reveals left facial droop, dysarthria, left hemiparesis (2/5 strength upper and lower extremities), and left sensory deficit. CT head without contrast shows no hemorrhage. CT perfusion reveals salvageable penumbra in the right MCA territory. tPA was administered followed by mechanical thrombectomy.
