A 78-year-old male with history of dementia presents from nursing home with fever, productive cough with yellow-green sputum, and increased confusion for 3 days. O2 sat 88% on room air. Physical exam reveals T 38.9°C, BP 110/70 mmHg, HR 98 bpm, RR 24/min. Decreased breath sounds and crackles in right lower lobe. Chest X-ray shows right lower lobe consolidation. Labs show WBC 15,000/μL with left shift, Na 128 mEq/L. Blood cultures drawn. Sputum culture grows Streptococcus pneumoniae. Patient diagnosed with healthcare-associated pneumonia.
